Your draft is already good. I would make it a little more polished, professional, and natural:

I really appreciate the opportunity and the trust from the team. I've genuinely enjoyed working with everyone and have had a very positive experience with both the team and the work.

At the moment, I do have some financial obligations and commitments with my current employer that I would like to honor, and I expect those commitments to be completed within the next 9–10 months.

After that, I'd certainly be open to discussing opportunities with CVS. For now, however, I would prefer to continue in my current contractor role while remaining fully committed to contributing to the team's success.

A few advantages of this version:

"Financial obligations and commitments" sounds more professional than just "financial obligation."
"Honor commitments" sounds positive and ethical.
"Open to discussing opportunities" keeps the door open without making a commitment.
"Fully committed to contributing" reassures the manager that you're not disengaging.

If you're saying this verbally on a call, I'd make it even more conversational:

"I really appreciate the opportunity and the confidence the team has shown in me. I've genuinely enjoyed working with everyone here. At the moment, I do have some commitments with my current employer that I'd like to honor, and those should be behind me in about 9 to 10 months. Once that's done, I'd definitely be open to having a conversation about opportunities with CVS. For now, I'd prefer to continue in my current contractor capacity while staying fully committed to supporting the team and our goals."

This sounds appreciative, professional, and future-oriented rather than a rejection.
