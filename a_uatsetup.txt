The primary downside of this approach is that it rewrites repository history, which changes commit hashes across branches and tags. This requires team members to re-sync or re-clone their local repositories and may impact active pull requests or references to historical commit IDs in Jira/PR discussions. There is also a need to carefully validate the replacement pattern to avoid unintended code/config changes. From a security standpoint, this is the most effective way to remove secrets from historical commits, but it should be executed with team communication and approval.
